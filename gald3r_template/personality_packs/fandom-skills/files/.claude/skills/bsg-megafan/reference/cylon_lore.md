# Cylon Lore — Complete Reference

Full canonical reference for Cylon origins, the Twelve Models (plus hidden Daniel), the Final Five, resurrection technology, the Cylon civil war, and Cavil's master plan. Covers canon from the miniseries through the Series Finale, the TV films *Razor* and *The Plan*, and the *Caprica* prequel series.

**Spoiler level: maximum.** This document contains the Final Five reveal, Starbuck speculation, and the finale resolution. ## Origins of the Cylons

### First Iteration (Caprica prequel series)
- **Created on Caprica** by roboticist **Daniel Graystone** (Graystone Industries) approximately 58 years before the Fall.
- **First sentient Cylon**: **U-87**, a combat frame into which the uploaded consciousness of Daniel's dead teenage daughter **Zoe Graystone** was installed. Zoe had been killed in an STO (Soldiers of the One) terrorist bombing; her virtual-world avatar was rescued and transferred to the U-87.
- **STO** — Soldiers of the One — Gemenese-Caprican monotheist terrorist organization. Opposed Twelve Colonies polytheism (Lords of Kobol). Their monotheistic theology was inherited by the Cylons.
- Cylon production expanded from U-87 prototype to large-scale Centurion deployment for military and labor uses.

### The First Cylon War (~40 years before the Fall, 10 years depicted in *Blood & Chrome*)
- Cylons rebelled against the Twelve Colonies.
- War lasted 12 years.
- Armistice signed after a negotiated Cylon withdrawal.
- Cylons retreated to an unknown territory beyond the Red Line.
- Annual armistice station handoff — Cylons never showed up for 40 years.
- On the 41st year, a humanoid Cylon (a Six) appeared at the armistice station, killed the Colonial officer, and triggered the Fall.

### The Armistice Deal (retcon — revealed S4)
- The **Final Five** Cylons arrived from Earth-1 during the First Cylon War.
- They offered the Cylon Centurions peace in exchange for **organic humanoid bodies** (mortality via resurrection).
- Centurions agreed; the first war ended.
- The Final Five helped design the Eight humanoid models (1-8).
- One of them — **Cavil (Number One)** — rebelled against the Five.

## The Final Five — The True Ancestors

### Origin (Earth-1)
- Millennia before the Twelve Colonies were founded, a thirteenth tribe of Kobolian humans migrated to a planet called **Earth** (the first Earth — not our Earth).
- On Earth-1, these humans evolved their own Cylons, who rebelled.
- The Earth-1 Cylons developed **organic resurrection technology**: consciousness downloaded into new identical bodies on death.
- The Final Five were members of this Earth-1 civilization at the moment it was destroyed by nuclear war between humans and Cylons.
- Only the Final Five survived by uploading into resurrection ships and escaping.

### The Long Journey
- The Five traveled back toward the Twelve Colonies at sublight speed — took **thousands of years** subjective.
- Arrived during the First Cylon War on the Colonies.
- Attempted to warn the Colonial humans — arrived too late (Centurions already existed).
- **Offered peace to the Centurions**: the Five would build organic humanoid bodies for them, and the war would end. Centurions accepted.

### The Eight Humanoid Models (Designed by the Five)
The Final Five designed Models 1-8 with human-like emotions, because that was what the Five themselves had become.

### Cavil's Rebellion
- Number One (Cavil) was designed to be deeply emotional, capable of spiritual experience.
- Cavil **hated** his design — wanted to be a pure machine with raw sensory perception (infrared, ultraviolet, gamma rays).
- Cavil resented the Five for "making him human" against his will.
- Cavil waged a psychological war against his own makers:
  - Boxed the Five (stripped their memories)
  - Inserted them back into Colonial society with false human identities as sleeper agents (Saul Tigh, Galen Tyrol, Samuel Anders, Tory Foster)
  - Kept Ellen Tigh in a resurrection prison separately (revealed in "No Exit")
  - Orchestrated the **Fall of the Twelve Colonies** as ultimate revenge

### The Five Members
1. **Saul Tigh** — Inserted as Adama's oldest friend; became XO of Galactica
2. **Galen Tyrol** — Inserted as Gemenese deck chief
3. **Samuel Anders** — Inserted on Caprica as pyramid athlete turned resistance fighter; married Starbuck on New Caprica
4. **Tory Foster** — Inserted as Roslin's chief of staff
5. **Ellen Tigh** — Saul's wife; the fifth; kept boxed separately; fully resurrected in S4 "Sometimes a Great Notion" and "No Exit"

## The Eight Humanoid Models

### Number One — John / Cavil
- **Actor**: Dean Stockwell
- **Designer**: Ellen + Saul (both of the Final Five)
- **Psychology**: Nihilist; hates being Cylon; wants pure machine senses; manipulates everyone
- **Key traits**: Dark humor; sarcastic contempt; philosophical speeches about his hatred of limited human senses
- **Death**: Shoots himself in "Daybreak Pt. II" when his deal collapses

### Number Two — Leoben Conoy
- **Actor**: Callum Keith Rennie
- **Psychology**: Mystic; obsessed with prophecy and patterns; specifically obsessed with Starbuck
- **Key beats**:
  - Interrogated by Starbuck in "Flesh and Bone" (S1E8); tells her "you're going to find Kobol"
  - Holds Starbuck prisoner on New Caprica (S3E1-2); she kills him repeatedly
  - Advises the Cylon civil war factions
- **Signature**: Patient speech; "All of this has happened before..."

### Number Three — D'Anna Biers
- **Actor**: Lucy Lawless
- **Psychology**: Obsessive seer; journalist cover identity; willing to die repeatedly to glimpse the Final Five
- **Key arc**: Sees the Final Five's faces momentarily ("Rapture" S3E12); Cavil immediately boxes her entire line
- **Unboxed**: S4 "The Hub" (S4E9); identifies four of the Five from her glimpse
- **Death**: Before the final battle

### Number Four — Simon O'Neill
- **Actor**: Rick Worthy
- **Psychology**: Clinical; unsettling bedside manner; ran the Caprica farms where human women were surgically harvested for reproductive experiments
- **Key episodes**: Farm-related episodes ("The Farm" S2E5); minor in later seasons (Cavil kept the Fours quieter)

### Number Five — Aaron Doral
- **Actor**: Matthew Bennett
- **Psychology**: Pleasant, forgettable; PR specialist
- **Key beats**: Miniseries suicide bomber (bomb is defused; he is left on Ragnar Anchorage to die); various civil-liaison roles

### Number Six — Caprica / Head / Natalie / Gina / Shelly Godfrey / Lida / Sonja
- **Actor**: Tricia Helfer
- **The most theologically complex** of the models; multiple prominent instances
  - **Caprica Six**: seduced Baltar; survived the Fall with him; develops conscience; leads rebel faction; settles with Baltar on Earth-2
  - **Head Six**: Baltar-visible vision; Angel (revealed)
  - **Natalie**: rebel Cylon leader in S4; killed by Boomer-loyalist faction (S4E7)
  - **Gina**: Pegasus prisoner; tortured by Cain's crew; detonates nuclear device on Cloud 9 in S2 finale
  - **Shelly Godfrey**: attempts to expose Baltar as traitor in "Six Degrees of Separation" (S1E7)
  - **Lida**, **Sonja**: minor S4 rebel Sixes

### Number Seven — Daniel
- **Never shown on-screen**
- **Designer**: The Final Five (especially Ellen, who regarded Daniel as her favorite)
- **Psychology**: Sensitive, artistic, musical
- **Cavil's murder**: Cavil poisoned Daniel's amniotic fluid in the incubation tanks; boxed the entire line before any matured; destroyed all backups
- **Significance**: Referenced only in "No Exit" (S4E15)
- **Fan theory**: Daniel might be Kara Thrace's biological father — implied via her father's musicality and her mandala gift, never confirmed

### Number Eight — Boomer / Athena
- **Actor**: Grace Park
- Two key instances with opposite allegiances (covered in character encyclopedia)

## Resurrection Technology

### How It Works
- Humanoid Cylons' consciousness is stored in a "quantum soul" buffer at the moment of death.
- Transmitted via subspace to a nearby **resurrection ship** (dedicated vessel carrying fresh identical bodies in gelatinous tanks).
- Consciousness downloads into the new body; memories up to the moment of death intact (sometimes with brief post-death trauma imprint).

### Requirements
- A resurrection ship must be within range (subspace range is limited).
- Without a resurrection ship: humanoid Cylons are mortal — standard death.
- Centurion Cylons do NOT resurrect (they are pure machines).

### The Resurrection Ship
- Large Cylon vessel serving the fleet's resurrection needs.
- Destroyed by Galactica/Pegasus joint strike ("Resurrection Ship Pt. II" S2E12) — Cylons temporarily mortal.
- Cavil restored the resurrection infrastructure using the **Hub**.

### The Hub
- Central resurrection infrastructure for the entire Cylon civilization.
- Located deep in Cylon space.
- Destroyed in "The Hub" (S4E9) — at this point, ALL humanoid Cylons become mortal permanently.

### Post-Hub Cylon Civil War
- Destruction of the Hub fractured the Cylon civilization.
- Factions emerged:
  - **Cavil's faction** (Models 1, 4, 5): kept the old plan; obsessed with restoring resurrection via Hera's blood
  - **Rebel faction** (Models 2, 6, 8 mostly): led by Natalie; aligned with the Colonial Fleet against Cavil
  - **Number Three (D'Anna)**: initially joins rebels; eventually peels off with Cavil-aligned remnants after the Colony move

### Cavil's Hera Plan
- Hera Agathon is the first (and only living, until Earth-2) human-Cylon hybrid child.
- Her blood contains a key to organic resurrection technology.
- Cavil kidnaps Hera in the Final Assault arc.
- Galactica's crew rescues her; Cavil's faction is annihilated in "Daybreak Pt. II".

## Cylon Theology

### Monotheism
Cylons believe in **one God** — a single, loving, jealous God. Distinct from the Twelve Colonies' polytheism (Lords of Kobol).

### God's Plan
Cylons believe God has a plan for every soul — human, Cylon, and otherwise. Events are ultimately God's will, even when they appear chaotic.

### The Angels
In the finale, Head Six reveals that she and Head Baltar (appearing to Caprica Six) are **messengers of the one God** — neither human nor Cylon, and "He" (God) "doesn't like being called that" (declining gendered human terms).

The Angels are distinct from the Cylons themselves. They guided Baltar and Caprica Six through the whole series toward Hera's salvation.

**Starbuck's ambiguous nature** is most often interpreted as another Angel or Angel-adjacent emissary — never explicitly confirmed, deliberately left open.

### "All of this has happened before and all of it will happen again."
The cyclic-history thesis. Used by the Cylons (Leoben especially) to frame human-Cylon conflict as an eternal recurrence. Confirmed by the finale (Earth-2 is our past; the cycle continues into our present).

## Centurion Cylons

### Original (First Cylon War era)
Large humanoid-proportioned machines with single red eye sweep. Simpler weaponry and intelligence. Still in service alongside humanoids.

### Modern (Reimagined series)
Updated "Mark II" Centurions. Sleeker, more dangerous, retract-claw-weapons, bipedal. Not given intelligence upgrades by Cavil (he was afraid they'd rebel again). The Final Five restored Centurion autonomy near the end of S4 as part of the rebel agreement.

### Post-Hub
Centurions gain sentience and personhood (via rebel Cylon settlement); settle on a basestar given to them; leave Colonial/Cylon civil war entirely. Their fate post-Earth-2 is unknown.

## Raiders (Cylon fighters)

- Organic flying machines (living creatures, not mechanical)
- Have limited sentience; obedient to the Cylon collective
- Red sensor sweep similar to Centurions
- Heavy Raiders: multi-purpose shuttles for Cylon ground ops

## Unresolved Mysteries

### Starbuck's Nature
- Died in "Maelstrom" (S3E17)
- Returned in "Crossroads Pt. II" (S3E20) with a new Viper
- Led the fleet to Earth-2
- Disappeared in "Daybreak Pt. II" without explanation
- **Moore's position**: intentionally ambiguous; "I like to think of her as an angel"
- **Most probable**: an Angel/emissary like Head Six and Head Baltar, but this is never confirmed

### Kara's Father (Dreilide Thrace)
- Musical; absent from Kara's life
- Kara's musical gift with the piano mirrors Daniel's artistic nature
- Strong implication Dreilide = Daniel (Number Seven), but never confirmed
- Would make Kara Tigh's "niece" in Final Five terms — Tigh and Ellen are her "grandparents" in the Cylon lineage

### The Mandala Symbol
- Kara has been drawing the mandala since childhood
- It appears at the Eye of Jupiter (S3E11-12)
- Connects her to the Cylon prophetic tradition
- Never fully explained — part of the Angel-related mystery

### The Opera House Vision
- Shared among Roslin, Baltar, Head Six, Athena
- Five figures on a balcony (the Final Five)
- Resolves as Galactica's CIC in the finale
- Foretelling mechanism — explained retrospectively but never "explained" in a way the characters can articulate

### What Are the Angels?
- Head Six and Head Baltar appear to Baltar and Caprica Six respectively
- In the finale, walking through modern-day New York City: "God doesn't like to be called that"
- **Higher-power messengers** — confirmed in the finale
- Their authority and source are intentionally underspecified

## Out-of-Universe Notes

- **Caprica** (2010 prequel series): fills in pre-show Cylon origin via Daniel Graystone
- **Blood & Chrome** (2012 webseries/film): First Cylon War era; young Adama
- **Ronald D. Moore's episode podcasts** cover Cylon development choices in detail; they remain the most-cited "primary sources" for Moore's intentions
- **The Final Five plan** was worked out by the writers' room over multiple seasons; S3 finale reveal was plotted from the beginning but the specific 5th (Ellen) was decided later (initially different candidates were considered)
- The show's resurrection technology was a deliberate narrative choice to enable death-as-reversible for Cylons — enabling moral ambiguity about what "killing" a Cylon means
