# Norse Mythology Character Encyclopedia — Classical Sources Only

> **Source policy:** All entries drawn exclusively from the Poetic Edda, Prose Edda (Snorri Sturluson), Icelandic Sagas, Skaldic poetry, and Saxo Grammaticus. NO Marvel/MCU content.

---

## THE AESIR

### Odin (Óðinn)
- **Epithets (heiti):** 200+ documented. Key: Allföðr (Allfather), Yggr (The Dreadful), Grímnir (Masked), Hroptr, Gautr, Bölverkr (Evil Worker), Wanderer, High One, Terrible One, Lord of the Gallows, God of the Hanged
- **Parents:** Borr and Bestla (a giantess, daughter of Bölthorn)
- **Siblings:** Vili and Vé (co-creators of the world and humanity)
- **Wife:** Frigg (chief wife); many liaisons with giantesses and others
- **Children:** Thor (by Jörð/Earth), Baldr and Höðr (by Frigg), Víðarr (by Gríðr), Váli (by Rindr), Hermóðr, Bragi (possibly), and many more
- **Hall:** Valaskjálf (contains Hliðskjálf, his high seat from which he sees all nine realms); Gladsheim (council hall)
- **Weapons/items:** Gungnir (spear, never misses, made by Sons of Ívaldi), Draupnir (ring replicating gold, gift back from Baldr's pyre), Sleipnir (eight-legged horse, born of Loki)
- **Companions:** Ravens Huginn and Muninn (fly daily over all worlds and report to him); wolves Geri and Freki (he feeds them all his food, drinking wine alone)
- **Self-sacrifice:** Hung on Yggdrasil, self-stabbed, nine nights, no food or drink → received knowledge of eighteen runes (Hávamál 138–141)
- **Eye sacrifice:** Gave left eye to drink from Mimir's Well for cosmic wisdom. The eye remains in the well.
- **Personality (classical):** Cunning, ruthless, strategically loving. Brings heroes to peak then arranges their deaths to populate Valhalla for Ragnarök. Does not break oaths (that would be dishonour) but finds ways around them.

### Thor (Þórr)
- **Parents:** Odin and Jörð (Earth, a giantess — making Thor half-giant)
- **Wife:** Sif (golden hair, grain goddess)
- **Mistress:** Járnsaxa (giantess); their sons: Magni and Móði
- **Son with Sif:** Þrúðr (a daughter, actually)
- **Hall:** Þrúðheimr / Þrúðvangr, with 540 rooms (Grímnismál)
- **Mjölnir details:** Handle too short (Brokkr's work was disrupted by a fly biting his eyelid — actually Loki in disguise). Must be worn with iron gloves (Járngreipr) and belt of strength (Megingjörð). When thrown, returns to hand. Used to consecrate marriages, births, and funeral pyres — Mjölnir is a symbol of sacred order as much as war.
- **Great journeys:** (1) To Útgarðr-Loki: the drinking-horn connected to the sea (he barely moved the level), the cat he lifted (Jörmungandr), the old woman he wrestled (Old Age — she took him to one knee). (2) Fishing trip with Hymir: caught Jörmungandr on a hook; Hymir cut the line in fear. (3) To retrieve his hammer as a disguised bride. (4) Journey to the giant Geirröðr (tricked by Loki, survived by gifts of a giantess Gríðr).
- **Þórr and Ragnarök:** Kills Jörmungandr with Mjölnir → walks nine steps → collapses from venom. His sons inherit Mjölnir.

### Loki
- **Father:** Fárbauti (a giant — "Dangerous Striker," possibly lightning)
- **Mother:** Laufey / Nál ("Needle") — possibly the first recorded matronymic; Loki often called "Laufeyjarson"
- **Brothers:** Helblindi, Býleistr
- **Blood-brother:** Odin (they mingled blood — this is why Loki is in Asgard despite being a giant)
- **Wife:** Sigyn (loyal; their sons Narfi and Váli; Váli transformed into a wolf who killed Narfi, whose entrails then bound Loki)
- **Partner:** Angrboða (giantess; Fenrir, Jörmungandr, Hel)
- **As a mare:** Loki transformed into a mare to lure away Svaðilfari (the giant builder's horse) during Asgard's wall construction; later gave birth to Sleipnir
- **Flyting (Lokasenna):** Accuses Odin of seiðr practice and gender transgression; accuses Freyr of giving away his sword; accuses Freyja of sleeping with all gods; reveals Tyr's wife was his lover; exposes Njörðr's incest origins among the Vanir; Bragi he calls cowardly. After Þór threatens him, he departs.
- **Binding:** In Hrafnkels saga, his son Váli (different Váli from Odin's) was transformed; in Gylfaginning, Narfi's entrails bind Loki to three pointed stones; Skadi hung a serpent above him; Sigyn catches the drops
- **Bound until Ragnarök**

### Frigg
- **Rank:** Highest-ranking goddess; sits beside Odin in Hliðskjálf
- **Hall:** Fensalir ("Fen Halls")
- **Handmaidens:** Fulla (shares her secret chest, carries her ashen box, knows her secrets), Gná (messenger, rides the horse Hófvarpnir), Hlín (protects those Frigg wishes saved), Sjöfn (concerned with love), Lofn (overcomes obstacles to marriages), Vár (oversees oaths and vows), Vör (wise, nothing hidden from her), Syn (guards hall doors against unlawful entry), Hlín (shields the beloved), Snotra (wise and gentle), Göndul (sometimes Valkyrie, sometimes handmaiden)
- **Knowledge:** Knows the fate of all beings. Never speaks it. This restraint is a defining characteristic.
- **Baldr's death:** Her attempt to prevent it (extracting oaths) is both the setup for the tragedy and her most significant canonical moment. She missed mistletoe because it was "too young" (Gylfaginning) or "too small" (interpretation varies).

### Baldr
- **Parents:** Odin and Frigg
- **Wife:** Nanna; she died of grief at his funeral and was burned with him on Hringhorni
- **Son:** Forseti (god of justice)
- **Hall:** Breiðablik ("Broad Gleaming") — no impurity may enter
- **Appearance:** Fairest and most beloved; his beauty described as shining; the whitest of flowers (Baldrs brá, "Baldr's eyelash") named after him
- **After Ragnarök:** Returns from Hel; reconciles with Höðr; together they rule in the new world

### Tyr (Týr)
- **Parentage:** Sometimes son of Odin; sometimes son of the giant Hymir (Hymiskviða) — the sources conflict
- **Hand:** His right hand is missing (bitten off by Fenrir)
- **Day:** Tuesday (Old English *Tīwesdæg*, from *Tīwaz* = Tyr)
- **Historical precedent:** Scholars believe Tyr (Germanic *Tīwaz*) was once the chief sky-deity before Odin superseded him; he retains the name cognate with Latin *Deus* and Greek *Zeus*

### Heimdall
- **Parents:** Nine mothers (all giantesses, all sisters — the nine daughters of Ægir)
- **Hall:** Himinbjörg ("Sky Cliffs") where Bifrost meets Asgard
- **Sensory gifts:** Sees 300 leagues in all directions; needs less sleep than a bird; hears grass growing, wool on sheep, and any sound across all nine realms
- **Horse:** Gulltoppr ("Gold-top")
- **Gjallarhorn:** When blown, summons all gods to Ragnarök; its sound heard across all nine realms. Also located at Mimir's Well (ambiguity in sources).
- **Rígsþula:** Heimdall as Rígr fathered Thrall (from Þír), Karl (from Móðir), and Jarl (from Faðir) — the three classes of Norse society. Jarl's son Konr Ungr ("Young Kin" = *konungr*, king) is the first true king.

### Freyr / Frey
- **Race:** Vanir, sent as hostage to Aesir
- **Hall/realm:** Álfheimr (gift from gods on his first tooth)
- **His items:** (1) Skíðblaðnir — largest ship in existence, folds to handkerchief-size, made by Sons of Ívaldi. (2) Gullinbursti — golden boar, faster than any horse, illuminates the night with its bristles. (3) His self-fighting sword — GIVEN AWAY to Skírnir as payment for wooing Gerðr. He will have no weapon at Ragnarök.
- **Gerðr:** The giantess Freyr fell in love with by glimpsing her from Hliðskjálf (Odin's seat — sitting there uninvited was itself transgressive). Her hands illuminated the sky and sea.
- **Ragnarök:** Killed by Surtr because he lacks his sword. Had the sword, he might have prevailed.

### Freyja
- **Race:** Vanir, sent as hostage to Aesir
- **Father:** Njörðr
- **Brother:** Freyr
- **Husband:** Óðr (largely absent — possibly Odin under another name, or a separate figure). She weeps tears of red gold for his absence. She searched for him across many lands, going by many names.
- **Hall:** Sessrúmnir ("Many-seated") in Fólkvangr
- **Animals:** Two cats (unnamed) pull her chariot; boar Hildisvíni
- **Falcon cloak:** Transforms the wearer into a falcon — she lent it to Loki on multiple occasions
- **Brísingamen:** A necklace of surpassing beauty made by four dwarves (Álfrigg, Dvalinn, Berlingr, Grerr). She spent four nights with each to obtain it. Loki stole it at Odin's direction (Sörla þáttr). Heimdall retrieved it.
- **Tears:** She weeps gold tears — this is the origin of gold in the earth (one mythological explanation)
- **Seiðr:** She is the chief practitioner and teacher of seiðr magic among the gods

### Njörðr
- **Race:** Vanir; chief of the Vanir hostages
- **Children:** Freyr and Freyja (by his unnamed sister — a practice apparently acceptable in Vanaheim, not among the Aesir)
- **Marriage:** Skadi. They tried alternating: nine nights in Þrymheimr (mountains), nine nights in Nóatún (Njörðr's sea-shore hall). Njörðr hated the wolves and mountains; Skadi hated the seabirds and sea noise. They separated.
- **Nóatún:** His hall; "Boat-enclosure" — on the sea-shore

### Bragi
- **Parentage:** Son of Odin (by the giantess Gunnlöð, according to some); earliest named skald-deity
- **Wife:** Idunn
- **Runes:** Carved on his tongue — the source of his eloquence
- **Historical Bragi:** May originally be a deified human skald, Bragi Boddason (c. 9th century)

### Idunn
- **Role:** Keeper of the golden apples (eski = apple-basket) that preserve the gods' youth
- **Kidnapping:** The giant Þjazi (in eagle form) forced Loki to lure Idunn outside Asgard's walls; Þjazi seized her. Without her apples, the gods grew grey and old. Loki retrieved her by borrowing Freyja's falcon cloak, turning Idunn into a nut, and flying back. Þjazi pursued as an eagle; the gods lit fires at Asgard's walls and burned his wings.

### Víðarr
- **Parents:** Odin and the giantess Gríðr
- **Attribute:** Extraordinary strength — second only to Thor. Wears a special shoe made from leather scraps.
- **Nature:** Silent; very few myths about him directly
- **Ragnarök:** Avenges Odin by killing Fenrir — either stabbing him through the palate with a sword, or holding his lower jaw down with one foot (the boot made of cobblers' offcuts) and tearing his upper jaw off with bare hands
- **Survival:** One of the gods who survives Ragnarök

### Váli (son of Odin)
- **Mother:** The giantess Rindr
- **Purpose:** Born specifically to avenge Baldr
- **Born full-grown:** On the day of his birth, he killed Höðr without washing his hands or combing his hair first
- **Survival:** One of the gods who survives Ragnarök

### Forseti
- **Parents:** Baldr and Nanna
- **Hall:** Glitnir — the roof is red gold, the pillars and walls are silver
- **Role:** All gods and men bring legal disputes to him; he is the best judge

### Höðr (Hodr)
- **Blindness:** Born blind; not malicious — simply unable to see what he was throwing
- **Death:** Killed by Váli (Odin's son, born for this purpose)
- **After Ragnarök:** Returns from Hel along with Baldr; they are reconciled

## VANIR (Additional)

### Skaði (Skadi)
- **Father:** Þjazi (giant killed by the gods when retrieving Idunn)
- **Race:** Giantess (jötunn) — one of the few giant/divine figures to be integrated into Asgardian society, though never truly Aesir
- **Marriage:** Chose Njörðr by his beautiful feet, thinking they were Baldr's
- **Domain:** Mountains, winter, skiing, hunting — she carved her home in her father's mountain hall Þrymheimr
- **Skiing:** Described as skiing through the mountains with a bow, hunting
- **Involvement in Loki's binding:** She hung the serpent above the bound Loki

### Gerðr
- **Race:** Giantess; daughter of Gymir and Aurboða
- **Appearance:** When she opened the door of her hall, air and sea shone with light from her arms
- **Significance:** Her eventual consent to meet Freyr in nine nights is the resolution of Skírnismál — the erotic and agricultural implications (the fertile land won for cultivation) are a common scholarly interpretation

## THE NORNS

### Urðr, Verðandi, Skuld
- **Location:** Well of Urðr at the base of Yggdrasil in Asgard
- **Roles:** Water the tree from the well and spread sacred clay on its roots; weave the threads of fate; carve runes on Yggdrasil's trunk
- **Urðr:** The eldest; the past; her name cognate with English "wyrd" (fate)
- **Verðandi:** The present; "that which is becoming"; the active process of being
- **Skuld:** The future; "that which shall be"; sometimes also appears as a Valkyrie — the debt to the future
- **Many Norns:** In addition to the three great Norns, individual norns attend each birth to assign personal fate. The quality of one's norns (noble vs. ill-favoured) explains good and bad fortune.

## SIGNIFICANT JOTNAR

### Ymir (Aurgelmir)
The primordial giant; formed from the meeting of fire and ice in Ginnungagap. Nourished by the cow Auðhumla. Sweated the first frost-giant couple from his armpit; his feet engendered a son together. Killed by Odin, Vili, and Vé; his body became the world.

### Surtr
- Fire giant; lord of Muspelheim; has a flaming sword
- **Ragnarök:** Leads the sons of Muspel; kills Freyr; sets fire to the entire world with his sword
- **Before the world:** Surtr was stationed at Muspelheim's border at the very beginning of creation (Völuspá)

### Hrungnir
The strongest of all giants (after Þjazi). Challenged Odin to a horse race; ended up in Asgard drinking. He and Thor fought in single combat at Grjótúnagard. Thor killed him with Mjölnir; Hrungnir's whetstone fragment embedded in Thor's forehead. Hrungnir fell on Thor, trapping him under his leg; only Thor's son Magni (still a young child) could lift it.

### Þjazi
- Kidnapped Idunn (as an eagle)
- Killed when the gods burned his wings at Asgard's wall
- Skadi's father; her vengeance began the negotiations that led to her integration into Asgard
- His eyes: Odin threw them into the sky and made them stars

### Útgarðr-Loki
The king of Útgarðr (the outer reaches of Jotunheim). Deceived Thor, Loki, and Þjálfi with illusions: drinking from a horn connected to the ocean, lifting the Midgard Serpent disguised as a cat, wrestling Old Age (Elli). When revealed, Útgarðr-Loki said if he had known how powerful Thor truly was, he would never have let him enter.

### Hrym
Giant helmsman of Naglfar (the ship of the dead) at Ragnarök; carries a shield

### Bergelmir and wife
The only giants to survive Ymir's death (his blood was the flood). They became the ancestors of all subsequent Jotnar.

## DWARVES (Dvergar)

### Sindri (Eitri) and Brokkr
Forged the three supreme treasures under wager with Loki:
1. **Gullinbursti** — golden boar for Freyr
2. **Draupnir** — gold ring for Odin (produces eight equal rings every ninth night)
3. **Mjölnir** — hammer for Thor (handle too short because Loki as a fly bit Brokkr's eyelid while he worked the bellows — he flinched briefly)

The gods judged Mjölnir the most valuable of all gifts; Loki lost the wager. Brokkr wanted to cut off Loki's head; Loki said he'd given his neck, not his head — so Brokkr sewed his lips shut instead.

### Sons of Ívaldi
Forged Gungnir (Odin's spear), Skíðblaðnir (Freyr's ship), and Sif's replacement golden hair.

### Brokk's Four (Álfrigg, Dvalinn, Berlingr, Grerr)
Made Freyja's Brísingamen; each received one night with her as payment.

### Andvari
A dwarf who lived as a pike in a pool. Tricked by Loki into giving up his gold hoard, including the ring Andvaranaut. Andvari cursed the ring to bring death to all who possessed it. This curse runs through the Völsung cycle (Hreiðmar, Fáfnir, Sigurd, and beyond).

## COSMIC BEINGS AND CREATURES

### Níðhöggr (Nidhogg)
- The corpse-gnawer; a serpent/dragon at the base of Yggdrasil in Hvergelmir
- Gnaws the root of Yggdrasil throughout all of time — its gnawing is a slow entropy threatening the cosmos
- Also gnaws the corpses of the wicked in Náströnd
- At Ragnarök it flies away carrying corpses in its wings — its survival into the new world suggests evil's persistence

### Ratatoskr
The squirrel running the trunk of Yggdrasil, carrying messages (insults) between the eagle at the crown and Níðhöggr at the roots. An agent of discord — the very structure of the cosmos includes a gossip.

### Jörmungandr (the Midgard Serpent / World Serpent)
- Son of Loki and Angrboða
- Cast into the ocean encircling Midgard by Odin; grew so large it encircles the world and bites its own tail
- Raised three times against Thor; they are destined to kill each other at Ragnarök

### Fenrir
- Son of Loki and Angrboða; raised in Asgard until he grew too dangerous
- **First chain:** Leyding — broke it easily
- **Second chain:** Drómi — broke it with effort
- **Third chain:** Gleipnir — made by the dwarves from: the sound of a cat's footstep, the beard of a woman, the roots of a mountain, the sinews of a bear, the breath of a fish, the spittle of a bird (all things that do not exist)
- **Þyr's hand:** He agreed to be bound if a god placed his hand in his mouth. Tyr did. Fenrir found he could not escape and bit Tyr's hand off.
- **Bound at:** Gioll rock, on Lyngvi island, in the lake Ámsvartnir; a sword propped in his mouth (blade up)
- **Ragnarök:** Breaks free; swallows Odin; killed by Víðarr

### Hel
- Daughter of Loki and Angrboða; cast into Niflheim by Odin to rule over the non-battle dead
- **Appearance:** Half her body is the colour of living flesh; half is that of a corpse (blue-black or green)
- **Hall:** Éljúðnir ("Sprayed with Snowstorms"); her dish is Hungr (Hunger), her knife Sultr (Famine), her threshold Fallandaforad (Danger of Stumbling), her bed Kör (Illness), her bed-curtains Blíkjandaböl (Gleaming Misfortune)
- **Role:** Neither cruel nor kind — simply the receiver of the dead. She agreed to release Baldr if every being in the world wept for him.

### Sleipnir
- Eight-legged horse; fastest in all nine realms; can travel to Hel and back
- **Birth:** Loki as mare mated with the stallion Svaðilfari; Sleipnir was born; Loki gave him to Odin
- **Used for:** Hermóðr's ride to Hel; Odin's wandering between worlds

### Huginn and Muninn
- Odin's two ravens; fly over all nine realms each morning and return to whisper news in his ears
- **Huginn** = Thought; **Muninn** = Memory
- Odin worries more for Muninn than Huginn (Hávamál implies Memory is more precious than Thought)

### Geri and Freki
- Odin's two wolves; "Greedy" and "Ravenous"
- Odin feeds all his food to them and drinks only wine (mead/wine = his sole sustenance)

### Gullinbursti
Freyr's golden boar; forged by Sindri. Its golden bristles illuminate the night. Faster than any horse.

### Auðhumla
The primordial cow; formed from melting ice alongside Ymir. Nourished Ymir with four rivers of milk. Herself nourished by licking salty ice blocks — she licked free the first man, Búri (ancestor of Odin), over three days.

### The Eagle of Yggdrasil (unnamed)
Sits at the crown of Yggdrasil. Hostile to Níðhöggr below. The hawk Veðrfölnir sits between his eyes. Ratatoskr carries their mutual insults.
