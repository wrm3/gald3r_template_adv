# Star Trek Character Encyclopedia

Comprehensive profiles across TOS / TAS / TNG / DS9 / VOY / ENT / DIS / PIC / LDS / PRO / SNW / films. Organized by series. Each profile: background, psychology, signature traits, key arc, iconic quotes, episode citations.

---

## TOS — The Original Series (2266-2269)

### James T. Kirk
- **Played by**: William Shatner
- **Born**: 2233, Riverside, Iowa (Prime); 2233 shuttle over *Kelvin* (Kelvin)
- **Middle name**: Tiberius (maternal grandfather)
- **Rank**: Captain, USS Enterprise (NCC-1701); later Rear Admiral (films)
- **Background**: Youngest captain in Starfleet history at his appointment. Fought in multiple campaigns. Beat the Kobayashi Maru by reprogramming the simulation ("I don't believe in a no-win scenario").
- **Psychology**: Decisive, passionate, bends rules when mission demands. The "three-man argument" with Spock (logic) and McCoy (emotion) is his decision-making method.
- **Signature**: Ripped uniform shirts during fights; dramatic pauses in speech; charm-offensive diplomacy (romanced adversaries into allies repeatedly)
- **Key arcs**: TOS → *Wrath of Khan* (losing Spock) → *Search for Spock* (recovering Spock) → *Voyage Home* (whales) → *Generations* (death in the Nexus)
- **Quotes**: "Risk is our business." "I don't believe in the no-win scenario." "KHAAAAAN!" "Beam me up, Scotty." (never exactly said this way)
- **Episodes**: TOS (entire series), films I-VII, *Generations*
- **Kelvin Kirk** (Chris Pine): younger, more brash; 2009, *Into Darkness*, *Beyond*

### Spock
- **Played by**: Leonard Nimoy; Zachary Quinto (Kelvin); Ethan Peck (SNW)
- **Born**: 2230, Shi'Kahr, Vulcan
- **Rank**: First Officer → Captain (Enterprise-A) → Ambassador (TNG)
- **Parents**: Sarek (Vulcan ambassador, Mark Lenard) and Amanda Grayson (human teacher, Jane Wyatt / Winona Ryder Kelvin / Mia Kirshner SNW)
- **Siblings**: Sybok (*ST V*), Michael Burnham (DIS, adopted)
- **Background**: Half-human, half-Vulcan; chose Starfleet over the Vulcan Science Academy (severing relationship with Sarek for 18 years). Best friend Kirk; brother to McCoy in banter.
- **Psychology**: Outwardly logical; internally conflicted about human heritage. Pon farr every 7 years (betrothed to T'Pring — see TOS "Amok Time"). Fal-tor-pan reunites his katra (TSFS).
- **Signature**: Raised eyebrow (~single eyebrow, precisely); "Fascinating." "Highly illogical." "Live long and prosper" + Vulcan salute. Nerve pinch (Vulcan neck pinch). Mind meld: "My mind to your mind, my thoughts to your thoughts."
- **Key arcs**: TOS → dies in *Wrath of Khan* → resurrected on Genesis planet (*Search for Spock*) → Ambassador to Romulus (TNG "Unification") → Kelvin timeline Spock Prime (2009 film, *Beyond*)
- **Death in Prime timeline**: Hobus supernova (2387), sacrificed himself attempting to contain it; pulled into black hole → Kelvin timeline
- **Episodes**: TOS (entire), TAS, films I-VI, TNG "Unification", DIS S2, SNW (young Spock), PIC S2 mentioned
- **SNW Young Spock**: Pre-TOS; more emotional; relationship with T'Pring and Christine Chapel

### Leonard "Bones" McCoy
- **Played by**: DeForest Kelley; Karl Urban (Kelvin)
- **Born**: 2227, Atlanta, Georgia
- **Rank**: Chief Medical Officer, Lieutenant Commander → Commander
- **Background**: Divorced before joining Enterprise; daughter Joanna (rarely seen); Southern country doctor roots.
- **Psychology**: Emotional counterweight to Spock's logic; deeply humanist; afraid of transporters; sarcastic defense against trauma.
- **Signature**: "Dammit Jim, I'm a doctor, not a ____!" (catalog of variants); "He's dead, Jim."; "I'm a doctor, not a mechanic/bricklayer/escalator/physicist/magician/miracle worker"
- **Key arcs**: Carried Spock's katra (*Wrath of Khan* → *Search for Spock*); briefly loses his mind from the burden. Admiral by TNG era; cameo in TNG pilot "Encounter at Farpoint" (S1E1) at age 137.
- **Quotes**: "Space — the final frontier. These are the voyages..." (narration sometimes given to him); "You green-blooded, inhuman..." (to Spock, affectionate)
- **Episodes**: TOS (entire), TAS, films I-VI, TNG S1E1

### Montgomery "Scotty" Scott
- **Played by**: James Doohan; Simon Pegg (Kelvin)
- **Born**: 2222, Linlithgow, Scotland
- **Rank**: Chief Engineer, Lieutenant Commander
- **Background**: Served in multiple ships pre-Enterprise. Writes/reads technical journals for fun. Pads estimates by 4x so he always looks like a miracle worker.
- **Psychology**: Scottish engineering pride; romantic (always pining for someone); protective of the warp core ("My bairns!"). Not really "I canna change the laws of physics" — he constantly does.
- **Signature**: Heavy Scottish accent; "She'll hold, Captain!"; love affair with Enterprise; throws punches in bar brawls ("Klingons are scum! But they do know how to fight.")
- **Key arcs**: Trapped in transporter buffer for 75 years ("Relics", TNG S6E4); rescued by Enterprise-D. Killed in *Star Trek* (2009) Kelvin.
- **Quotes**: "I'm giving her all she's got, Captain!" "It's green." (referring to Vulcan drink); "Ach, Captain, I dinnae think she can take much more!"

### Nyota Uhura
- **Played by**: Nichelle Nichols; Zoe Saldaña (Kelvin); Celia Rose Gooding (SNW)
- **Rank**: Lieutenant (TOS), later Commander
- **Background**: Polyglot; Swahili speaker; exceptional ear for language. Nichelle Nichols personally recruited by MLK to stay on TOS as one of the first Black women in a professional-equal role on US television.
- **Signature**: Calm professional hails; earpiece; singing (occasionally); cryptographic linguistic work
- **Key moments**: First interracial kiss on US TV (TOS "Plato's Stepchildren", S3E10). Holds Spock's hand in her recovery ("Search for Spock"). Saves the day in *Undiscovered Country* (Klingon dictionary scene).
- **SNW Young Uhura**: Cadet aboard Enterprise; still finding her career; strong musical friendships (notably with Hemmer pre-his-death).

### Hikaru Sulu
- **Played by**: George Takei; John Cho (Kelvin)
- **Rank**: Helmsman → Captain, USS Excelsior (*Undiscovered Country*)
- **Background**: Botany hobbyist (TOS "The Man Trap"); fencing enthusiast (TOS "The Naked Time" shirtless rapier scene); daughter Demora is helm officer of Enterprise-B in *Generations*.
- **Signature**: Calm competence; dry humor; ship command
- **Kelvin**: out gay with partner (one of first major SF franchise gay male heroes)

### Pavel Chekov
- **Played by**: Walter Koenig; Anton Yelchin (Kelvin, †2016)
- **Rank**: Ensign → Commander (films)
- **Background**: Added S2 partly to appeal to young audiences (Davy Jones–style); Russian pride running gag ("was invented in Russia"). Yelchin's sudden death (2016 car accident) led to *Beyond*'s in-memoriam dedication.
- **Signature**: "Keptin!"; 'v' pronounced as 'w'; youthful enthusiasm; navigating

### Christine Chapel
- **Played by**: Majel Barrett (TOS, also Lwaxana Troi in TNG/DS9, plus Enterprise computer voice in most series); Jess Bush (SNW)
- **Rank**: Nurse → Doctor
- **Background**: In TOS, in love with Dr. Roger Korby (found on Exo III); unrequited feelings for Spock. In SNW, younger, pre-doctorate, complicated with Spock.

### M'Benga
- **Played by**: Booker Bradshaw (TOS); Babs Olusanmokun (SNW)
- **Rank**: Doctor
- **Background**: Trained on Vulcan; knows Vulcan physiology better than most humans. SNW establishes war trauma from Klingon conflict; "Under the Cloak of War" (SNW S2E8) is M'Benga-centric.

### Khan Noonien Singh
- **Played by**: Ricardo Montalbán (TOS + *Wrath of Khan*); Benedict Cumberbatch (Kelvin *Into Darkness*)
- **Background**: Genetic superhuman from the Eugenics Wars (1990s in original continuity, pushed later via retcon to ~2020s). Found in stasis aboard *SS Botany Bay* ("Space Seed" TOS S1E22). Marooned on Ceti Alpha V with his followers; returned 15 years later in *Wrath of Khan* (after Ceti Alpha VI exploded and his wife died) for revenge.
- **Signature**: Shakespeare quotes (especially Moby Dick in *Wrath of Khan*); bare chest; obsessive vengeance
- **Quotes**: "From hell's heart I stab at thee!" "KHAAAAN!" (Kirk's response) "Revenge is a dish that is best served cold — it is very cold in space."

### Sarek
- **Played by**: Mark Lenard; Ben Cross (Kelvin); James Frain (DIS)
- **Background**: Vulcan ambassador; father of Spock, Sybok (*ST V*), and adopted daughter Michael Burnham (DIS). Three wives (Vulcan first wife, Amanda Grayson, Perrin in TNG).

## TNG — The Next Generation (2364-2370)

### Jean-Luc Picard
- **Played by**: Patrick Stewart
- **Born**: 2305, La Barre, France
- **Rank**: Captain (Enterprise-D, Enterprise-E) → Admiral (retired, PIC)
- **Background**: Joined Starfleet despite family's vineyard tradition. Stabbed through the heart by Nausicaans as a cadet (replaced with artificial heart, "Samaritan Snare" and "Tapestry" TNG S6E15). Commanded *USS Stargazer* for 22 years before Enterprise-D. Grandson of Maurice Picard.
- **Psychology**: Philosopher-diplomat first, warrior last. Quotes Shakespeare and French literature. Archeology hobbyist. PTSD from Borg assimilation.
- **Key arcs**:
  - Made Locutus of Borg ("Best of Both Worlds" TNG S3E26/S4E1)
  - Lived an entire life on Kataan in 25 minutes ("The Inner Light" TNG S5E25); plays the Ressikan flute in remembrance
  - "There are FOUR lights!" ("Chain of Command, Part II" TNG S6E11) — torture resistance
  - Q's final test ("All Good Things..." TNG S7E25-26)
  - Son Jack Crusher revealed (PIC S3)
  - Dies → downloaded into android body (PIC S1 finale)
- **Signature**: "Tea, Earl Grey, hot."; "Make it so."; "Engage."; uniform tug (Picard Maneuver, fan-named); the Ressikan flute
- **Quotes**: "The line must be drawn HERE!" (*First Contact*); "Things are only impossible until they're not."

### Data
- **Played by**: Brent Spiner (also plays Lore, B-4, and the Soong ancestors)
- **Activation**: 2336, Omicron Theta
- **Creator**: Dr. Noonien Soong (and Dr. Juliana Soong, Data's "mother")
- **Background**: Android; positronic brain; 100TB memory; attempts to be human. Brother Lore (evil, emotion-capable); simpler precursor B-4; "daughter" Lal ("The Offspring" TNG S3E16, she dies shortly after creation).
- **Psychology**: Literal; curious; without affect until the emotion chip (*Generations*). **Does not use contractions.** ("I am functioning" never "I'm functioning.") One exception: "First Contact" when contractions begin to creep in post-emotion-chip; rare.
- **Signature**: Head-tilts; complete lack of contractions; pet cat Spot; violin; poetry ("Ode to Spot"); tried dating (Jenna D'Sora "In Theory" TNG S4E25)
- **Key arcs**:
  - Personhood trial ("The Measure of a Man" TNG S2E9)
  - Lal ("The Offspring")
  - Emotion chip installed (*Generations* → *First Contact* → *Insurrection* → *Nemesis*)
  - Dies sacrificing himself for Picard (*Nemesis*)
  - Consciousness resurrected in PIC S1 finale / S3 (as himself in a final android body)
- **Quotes**: "I am fully functional." (to Yar in "The Naked Now"); "Intriguing." (occasionally) "Curious." (frequently)

### William T. Riker
- **Played by**: Jonathan Frakes
- **Rank**: Commander (Enterprise-D) → Captain (USS Titan) → Admiral (PIC S3)
- **Background**: Alaskan; father Kyle Riker estranged. Second-in-command who repeatedly declined his own command offers out of loyalty to Picard. Thomas Riker (transporter-duplicate from "Second Chances" TNG S6E24) is an alternate path.
- **Signature**: Trombone playing; beard (grown for S2, rarely removed); the Riker Maneuver (chair-swing, Frakes's back injury workaround); "Number One" nickname (Picard's)
- **Key arcs**: Thomas Riker, marriage to Troi (*Nemesis*), Titan command (PIC S3)

### Deanna Troi
- **Played by**: Marina Sirtis
- **Background**: Half-Betazoid; mother Lwaxana Troi (Majel Barrett); father Ian Andrew Troi (human). Empathic (senses emotions); not full telepathy (half-human).
- **Rank**: Counselor → Commander
- **Signature**: Sensing deception; chocolate sundae coping mechanism; purple/lavender off-duty fashion; eventually earns command via the Bridge Officer's Test ("Thine Own Self" TNG S7E16)

### Worf
- **Played by**: Michael Dorn
- **Born**: 2340, Qo'noS
- **Rank**: Lieutenant → Lt. Commander → Ambassador to Kronos → Commander (PIC S3)
- **Background**: Orphaned at Khitomer (Romulan attack, 2346). Raised by human adoptive parents Sergei and Helena Rozhenko. Estranged from Klingon Empire (accepted discommendation over his father's falsely accused treason, TNG "Sins of the Father").
- **Son**: Alexander Rozhenko (with K'Ehleyr, who is killed by Duras).
- **Signature**: "Today is a good day to die." Growling. Klingon opera. Prune juice ("a warrior's drink"). Bat'leth master.
- **Key arcs**:
  - Discommendation arc (TNG S3E17 → S4 "Redemption")
  - K'Ehleyr and Alexander
  - DS9 — marries Jadzia Dax, widowed (DS9 S6E26 "Tears of the Prophets"); adopts Worf of House Martok as his house head
  - Kills Gowron, installs Martok as Chancellor (DS9 S7E22 "Tacking Into the Wind")
  - PIC S3 — mentorship of Raffi, philosophical calm

### Geordi La Forge
- **Played by**: LeVar Burton
- **Rank**: Lieutenant → Lt. Commander (Chief Engineer)
- **Background**: Born blind (congenital); wears VISOR; replaced with ocular implants in *First Contact*. Later wife Leah Brahms (daughters mentioned in PIC S3).
- **Signature**: VISOR; engineering optimism; Reg Barclay mentorship; holodeck friendship with Data

### Beverly Crusher
- **Played by**: Gates McFadden
- **Rank**: Chief Medical Officer, Commander
- **Background**: Widowed (husband Jack Crusher killed on *Stargazer* under Picard's command); son Wesley Crusher.
- **Key arcs**: Nearly romance with Picard throughout TNG ("Attached" TNG S7E8); PIC S3 reveals son Jack Crusher with Picard.

### Wesley Crusher
- **Played by**: Wil Wheaton
- **Rank**: Acting Ensign → Cadet → (left Starfleet)
- **Background**: Child prodigy; The Traveler took him off to explore higher planes (TNG "Journey's End" S7E20). Returns in PRO finale as an interdimensional traveler.

### Tasha Yar
- **Played by**: Denise Crosby
- **Background**: Security Chief S1; killed by Armus ("Skin of Evil" TNG S1E23). Returns via alternate timeline ("Yesterday's Enterprise" TNG S3E15); her alternate self is captured by Romulans, has daughter Sela (also Denise Crosby).

### Q
- **Played by**: John de Lancie
- **Background**: Member of Q Continuum (extradimensional omnipotent beings). Chose Picard as his subject / student / foil.
- **Key arcs**:
  - Introduces humanity to the Borg ("Q Who" TNG S2E16)
  - Tests Picard through time in "All Good Things..." (TNG S7E25-26)
  - Dying in PIC S2; final goodbye to Picard
- **Signature**: "Mon capitaine!"; snaps fingers to change reality; condescending affection

### Guinan
- **Played by**: Whoopi Goldberg; Ito Aghayere (young Guinan, PIC S2)
- **Background**: El-Aurian (species targeted by Borg); thousands of years old; friend of Picard from before TNG. Listens more than speaks. Husband (one of many through her long life) history scattered.
- **Signature**: Hat; Ten Forward bar; listens; drops wisdom sparingly

### Lwaxana Troi
- **Played by**: Majel Barrett
- **Background**: Troi's mother; Betazoid ambassador; daughter of the Fifth House, holder of the Sacred Chalice of Rixx, heir to the Holy Rings of Betazed. Pursues Picard constantly (eventually marries Homn the valet).

### Reginald "Reg" Barclay
- **Played by**: Dwight Schultz
- **Background**: Engineering officer with anxiety and holodeck addiction. Lt. junior grade; eventually helps Pathfinder contact Voyager (VOY "Pathfinder" S6E10).

### Lore
- **Played by**: Brent Spiner
- **Background**: Data's older "brother" android; built by Soong with full emotions; unstable; aligns with Borg ("Descent" TNG S6E26 / S7E1). Eventually disassembled; his neural net resurfaces in PIC S3.

### Ro Laren
- **Played by**: Michelle Forbes
- **Rank**: Ensign → Lt. Commander (PIC)
- **Background**: Bajoran; defected to the Maquis in TNG "Preemptive Strike" (S7E24). Returns in PIC S3 as a Starfleet Intelligence officer; dies on her ship.

## DS9 — Deep Space Nine (2369-2375)

### Benjamin Sisko
- **Played by**: Avery Brooks
- **Rank**: Commander → Captain
- **Background**: Widowed at Wolf 359 (wife Jennifer killed by Borg-Locutus fleet; he personally saw the commander responsible → Picard); son Jake. Later remarried to Kasidy Yates.
- **Emissary of the Prophets**: Bajoran religious figure who communicates with the wormhole aliens. Conceived by the Prophets via human mother Sarah.
- **Psychology**: Grief into steel will; a commander who understands both Starfleet idealism and frontier compromise. Baseball fan (the London Kings). Cook.
- **Signature**: Bald (shaved head from S4 onward); goatee (also from S4); "Old Man" (Dax); baseball; cooking for the crew
- **Key arcs**:
  - Dominion War
  - "In the Pale Moonlight" (DS9 S6E19) — engineers Romulan entry into war
  - Becomes the Emissary fully (DS9 S7E25-26 "What You Leave Behind"); joins the Prophets; promises Kasidy he'll return
- **Quotes**: "I can live with it. COMPUTER, erase that entire personal log." (DS9 S6E19)

### Kira Nerys
- **Played by**: Nana Visitor
- **Rank**: Major (Bajoran Militia) → Colonel → Captain (Defiant, DS9 finale)
- **Background**: Bajoran resistance fighter during Cardassian Occupation (since age 12). Parents both killed. Deeply religious (follows Prophets). Temperamentally sharp.
- **Key arcs**:
  - Befriends Odo; romantic relationship develops
  - Carries the O'Briens' baby (Kirayoshi) after Keiko's shuttle accident
  - Leads Bajoran resistance against Dominion
  - Takes command of DS9 at the end

### Odo
- **Played by**: René Auriberjonois
- **Rank**: Security Chief (Bajoran/Starfleet provisional)
- **Background**: Changeling; found by Bajoran scientist Dr. Mora Pol; one of the Hundred (Founders' scout population). Skeptical of solids' "love" but develops one with Kira.
- **Signature**: Must return to liquid state every 16-18 hours; limited shapeshifting ability (can't do perfect faces); dry disapproval of Quark's schemes (professional rivalry and mutual respect)
- **Key arcs**:
  - Forms with the Female Changeling (Great Link)
  - Temporarily stripped of changeling ability
  - Returns to the Great Link at DS9's end to cure the plague (infected by Section 31)

### Quark
- **Played by**: Armin Shimerman
- **Background**: Ferengi bar owner; brother Rom (inept but good-hearted); nephew Nog (first Ferengi in Starfleet). Mother Ishka (Moogie) reforms Ferengi women's rights.
- **Signature**: Rules of Acquisition in every scene; gold-pressed latinum obsession; ethical only when forced
- **Key arcs**:
  - Nog's Starfleet career
  - Moogie's reforms of Ferengi society
  - Unexpected heroism during Dominion War

### Jadzia Dax
- **Played by**: Terry Farrell
- **Background**: Trill, joined with Dax symbiont (7 previous hosts, including Curzon Dax who was Sisko's mentor — hence "Old Man"). Brilliant scientist, daring pilot, Klingon warrior-by-marriage.
- **Key arcs**:
  - Marries Worf
  - Killed by Dukat (possessed by Pah-wraith, DS9 S6E26 "Tears of the Prophets")

### Ezri Dax
- **Played by**: Nicole de Boer
- **Background**: New host for Dax symbiont after Jadzia's death; counselor; less prepared than Jadzia (emergency joining). Relationship with Bashir.

### Miles O'Brien
- **Played by**: Colm Meaney
- **Background**: Chief of Operations, former TNG transporter chief. Wife Keiko (botanist); daughter Molly; son Kirayoshi.
- **Key arcs**:
  - "Hard Time" (DS9 S4E18) — implanted with 20 years of false memories; PTSD
  - "Honor Among Thieves" (DS9 S6E15)
  - Teaching at Starfleet Academy post-DS9

### Julian Bashir
- **Played by**: Alexander Siddig (credited Siddig El Fadil early)
- **Background**: Chief Medical Officer; turned out to be genetically enhanced (parents illegally modified him as a child because he was failing school) — revealed "Doctor Bashir, I Presume?" (DS9 S5E16).
- **Signature**: Earnest enthusiasm; obsession with being a frontier doctor; secret super-intelligence revealed gradually; James Bond holodeck programs ("Our Man Bashir")

### Elim Garak
- **Played by**: Andrew Robinson
- **Background**: "Plain, simple Garak" — Cardassian exile, former Obsidian Order operative. Tailor aboard DS9. Son of Enabran Tain.
- **Signature**: Every statement has double meaning; "My dear doctor..."; lunches with Bashir; catastrophic honesty occasionally
- **Key arcs**:
  - "The Die Is Cast" (DS9 S3E21) — Obsidian Order collapse
  - "In the Pale Moonlight" (DS9 S6E19) — kills Vreenak and the forger so Sisko can have his war
  - Leads Cardassian resistance at series end

### Gul Dukat
- **Played by**: Marc Alaimo
- **Background**: Prefect of Bajor during Occupation. Daughter Ziyal (half-Bajoran, killed by Damar). Multi-season villain arc; goes from complicated antagonist to Pah-wraith-possessed agent of chaos.

### Kai Winn
- **Played by**: Louise Fletcher
- **Background**: Power-hungry Bajoran spiritual leader; becomes Kai; ultimately turns to the Pah-wraiths alongside Dukat; dies trying to release them.

### Weyoun
- **Played by**: Jeffrey Combs
- **Background**: Vorta, clones 1-8 serving the Dominion; sarcastic obsequiousness toward his Changeling gods.

### Martok
- **Played by**: J.G. Hertzler
- **Background**: Klingon general, one-eyed (eye lost to a Jem'Hadar); Worf's house leader; becomes Chancellor after Worf kills Gowron.

### Nog
- **Played by**: Aron Eisenberg
- **Background**: First Ferengi in Starfleet; Rom's son; loses leg in Dominion War ("The Siege of AR-558" DS9 S7E8); heartbreaking recovery arc.

### Rom
- **Played by**: Max Grodénchik
- **Background**: Quark's brother; engineering genius hidden by Ferengi stupidity expectations; becomes Grand Nagus at DS9's end (reforming Ferengi society).

## VOY — Voyager (2371-2378)

### Kathryn Janeway
- **Played by**: Kate Mulgrew
- **Rank**: Captain → Admiral (by *Nemesis*; future Admiral in "Endgame")
- **Background**: Scientist turned captain. Fiancé Mark and dog Molly left behind on Earth. Stranded in Delta Quadrant 2371-2378.
- **Psychology**: Iron will; scientific curiosity; willing to be ruthless to bring the crew home; destroyed Caretaker array to protect Ocampa (creating 70-year journey).
- **Signature**: "Coffee, black." The coffee obsession is canonical and load-bearing. Bun (S1-2) → shorter hair (S3+).
- **Key arcs**:
  - Alliance with Borg ("Scorpion") to defeat Species 8472
  - Year of Hell ("Year of Hell" S4E8-9)
  - Equinox — facing a darker alternate Starfleet captain
  - Endgame — future Janeway breaks time to shortcut Voyager home
  - Admiral in PRO, then uploads as hologram
- **Quotes**: "There's coffee in that nebula."

### Chakotay
- **Played by**: Robert Beltran
- **Rank**: Commander; former Maquis captain
- **Background**: Dorvan V; Native American heritage (show's consultant later revealed as fraudulent; production later acknowledged the gap). Left Starfleet for Maquis after Cardassian treaty gave his home to Cardassians.
- **Signature**: Animal guide/vision quest rituals; boxing; eventual romance with Seven of Nine (finale rushed)

### Seven of Nine
- **Played by**: Jeri Ryan
- **Background**: Annika Hansen, assimilated by Borg as a child (her parents were researchers studying the Borg); disconnected from the Collective by Janeway ("Scorpion" S3E26/S4E1).
- **Psychology**: Blunt efficiency; rediscovering humanity; developed mentor relationships with Naomi Wildman, Icheb (Borg children rescued in S5-6); romantic arc with Chakotay (rushed).
- **Signature**: Regeneration alcove; Borg implants (partial); catsuit costuming controversial; "Irrelevant." / "Resistance is futile." ironic usage
- **Key arcs**:
  - Unimatrix Zero arc (S6 finale / S7 premiere)
  - PIC era: older Seven is a Fenris Ranger, eventually Captain of Titan-A (PIC S3)
- **Quotes**: "I am no longer Borg."

### The Doctor (EMH Mark I)
- **Played by**: Robert Picardo
- **Background**: Emergency Medical Hologram; activated continuously after original medical staff killed in pilot. Develops into full person; names himself various things ("Schmullus", never settles).
- **Signature**: Opera singing; "Please state the nature of the medical emergency"; self-deprecating arrogance; hypochondriac self-diagnosis (despite being a hologram)
- **Key arcs**:
  - Mobile emitter from 29th century ("Future's End" S3E8) allows him to leave sickbay
  - Family program (Belle + son)
  - "Living Witness" (S4E23) — backup module aboard an alien ship, 700 years later

### Tuvok
- **Played by**: Tim Russ
- **Rank**: Lt. Commander → Lt. Commander (Chief of Security/Tactical Officer)
- **Background**: Vulcan; 100+ years old by VOY; served under Sulu on the Excelsior (VOY "Flashback" S3E2); has wife T'Pel and 4 children on Vulcan.
- **Signature**: Driest Vulcan in the franchise; Kolinahr training; meditations; teaches Kes mental discipline; friendship with Neelix (despite irritation)

### B'Elanna Torres
- **Played by**: Roxann Dawson
- **Rank**: Lieutenant (Chief Engineer)
- **Background**: Klingon-Human; rejected by Academy (dropped out); Maquis engineer before Voyager. Mother Miral Torres. Marries Tom Paris; daughter Miral.
- **Signature**: Klingon temper; brilliant engineer; self-hatred about Klingon heritage (resolved through "Barge of the Dead" S6E3 and other eps)

### Tom Paris
- **Played by**: Robert Duncan McNeill)
- **Rank**: Lieutenant (Helm)
- **Background**: Son of Admiral Owen Paris; disgraced Starfleet officer; brief Maquis stint; retro Americana obsessive (Captain Proton holodeck program).
- **Signature**: 1950s aesthetic; car restoration; Tom/B'Elanna relationship

### Harry Kim
- **Played by**: Garrett Wang
- **Rank**: Ensign (the entire series; joke)
- **Background**: Fresh Academy grad; Korean-American; parents wait 7 years for return.
- **Running gag**: Never promoted. Died multiple times in alternate timelines. "Timeless" (S5E6) — future Kim tries to save Voyager.

### Neelix
- **Played by**: Ethan Phillips
- **Background**: Talaxian; survivor of Haakonian attack (his entire family killed); moral officer aboard Voyager (self-appointed). Briefly husband-figure to Kes.
- **Signature**: Cooking (terrible); relentless optimism; "ambassador to everyone"
- **Departure**: Stays with Talaxian refugee colony (VOY S7E23 "Homestead").

### Kes
- **Played by**: Jennifer Lien
- **Background**: Ocampa; 2-year lifespan (species average ~9 years); psychic abilities; departs S4 to ascend to higher energy form. Returns darkly in "Fury" (S6E23).

### Naomi Wildman
- **Played by**: Scarlett Pomers
- **Background**: First Voyager baby; mother Samantha Wildman (Ensign); father Ktarian, left on DS9. Seven's closest friend.

### Icheb
- **Played by**: Manu Intiraymi
- **Background**: Former Borg drone rescued with other Borg children; stays aboard; goes to Starfleet Academy. Tortured and killed in PIC S1 (Bjayzl).

## ENT — Enterprise (2151-2161)

### Jonathan Archer
- **Played by**: Scott Bakula
- **Rank**: Captain (first NX-class)

## DIS — Discovery (2256-2259, then 3189+)

### Michael Burnham
- **Played by**: Sonequa Martin-Green
- **Rank**: Commander → Mutineer (imprisoned) → Science Specialist → Captain (S4+)
- **Background**: Parents killed by Klingons; adopted by Sarek and Amanda (Vulcan upbringing). Foster sister of Spock (DIS S2). Mutinied against Captain Georgiou in the pilot, starting the Federation-Klingon War.
- **Key arcs**:
  - Mutiny and redemption
  - Spore drive era; jumping to the 32nd century
  - Becomes Captain of Discovery

### Saru
- **Played by**: Doug Jones
- **Background**: Kelpien — prey species on home planet (Kaminar); threat ganglia detect approaching death. Vahar'ai transformation (DIS S2E6 "The Sound of Thunder") — no longer prey.
- **Key arcs**: First Kelpien in Starfleet; eventually Captain; romance with Ni'Var priestess T'Rina.

### Paul Stamets
- **Played by**: Anthony Rapp
- **Background**: Astromycologist; invented spore drive navigator (tardigrade-augmented DNA); husband of Hugh Culber.
- **Signature**: Prickly genius; mycelial network vision

### Hugh Culber
- **Played by**: Wilson Cruz
- **Background**: Ship's doctor; murdered by Voq/Tyler in S1; returns from mycelial network in S2; eventually becomes therapist for the crew.

### Sylvia Tilly
- **Played by**: Mary Wiseman
- **Background**: Ensign → Lieutenant; nervous brilliant; Burnham's closest friend; temporarily first officer; leaves to teach at the new Starfleet Academy (DIS S4).

### Cleveland "Book" Booker
- **Played by**: David Ajala
- **Background**: 32nd-century courier; empathic with his cat Grudge; lost his home planet Kwejian (DIS S4). Burnham's romantic partner.

### Philippa Georgiou
- **Played by**: Michelle Yeoh
- **Background**: Captain of *USS Shenzhou*, killed by Klingons in pilot. Mirror Universe Georgiou (Terran Emperor) becomes Section 31 operative; headlines *Section 31* (2025 film).

### Gabriel Lorca
- **Played by**: Jason Isaacs
- **Background**: Mirror Universe Lorca impersonating Prime Lorca; S1 twist. Eats fortune cookies. Killed by Georgiou.

### Ash Tyler / Voq
- **Played by**: Shazad Latif
- **Background**: Klingon (Voq) surgically altered into human (Ash Tyler) with implanted memories; identity fragmented. Eventually joins Section 31.

### Adira Tal
- **Played by**: Blu del Barrio
- **Background**: First Trill human host (late 32nd century adaptation); Gray's partner (Gray is the previous Tal host, initially holographic). Non-binary.

## SNW — Strange New Worlds (2259-2261)

### Christopher Pike
- **Played by**: Anson Mount
- **Rank**: Captain (Enterprise NCC-1701, pre-Kirk)
- **Background**: Knows his own tragic future from a Klingon time crystal (DIS S2); will be disabled and confined to wheelchair in "The Menagerie" (TOS S1E11-12). Chooses to continue anyway.
- **Signature**: Avuncular captaincy; cooking breakfasts for his officers; horses (Rigel VII ranch holodeck program)
- **Key episodes**: "A Quality of Mercy" (S1E10) — vision of his future if he saves a cadet; decides he cannot

### Young Spock
- Covered in TOS section; younger, more emotional, T'Pring-era SNW Spock.

### Una Chin-Riley (Number One)
- **Played by**: Rebecca Romijn
- **Rank**: Commander (first officer of Enterprise)
- **Background**: Illyrian — a genetically augmented species banned from Starfleet; hid her heritage. Court-martialed and cleared in "Ad Astra Per Aspera" (S2E2).

### La'an Noonien-Singh
- **Played by**: Christina Chong
- **Background**: Descendant of Khan Noonien Singh; raised in a Gorn prison colony. Security chief aboard Enterprise. Suppressed family shame.
- **Key episode**: "Tomorrow and Tomorrow and Tomorrow" (S2E3) — time travel romance with Kirk, must let him go to preserve timeline.

### Young Nyota Uhura
- **Played by**: Celia Rose Gooding
- **Background**: Cadet aboard Enterprise; still deciding Starfleet vs. civilian linguistics work; close friendship with Hemmer pre-his-death.

### Young Christine Chapel
- **Played by**: Jess Bush
- **Background**: Younger than TOS Chapel; less formally trained (pre-Dr.); complicated romantic history with Spock (ends badly per SNW S2).

### Hemmer
- **Played by**: Bruce Horak
- **Background**: Aenar engineer (blind species, cousin-race of Andorians). Dies in "All Those Who Wander" (S1E9) — season-defining loss. Telepathic.

### Erica Ortegas
- **Played by**: Melissa Navia
- **Background**: Pilot ("I fly the ship"); deadpan; Klingon War veteran. Underdeveloped in S1; S2 gives more backstory.

### Pelia
- **Played by**: Carol Kane
- **Background**: Lanthanite (long-lived species); former Academy instructor; joins Enterprise as Chief Engineer in S2. Knew Amelia Earhart personally (because centuries old).

## LDS — Lower Decks (2380-2382)

### Beckett Mariner
- **Played by**: Tawny Newsome
- **Rank**: Ensign (demoted and repromoted repeatedly) → Lieutenant JG (finale)
- **Background**: Daughter of Captain Carol Freeman (Cerritos captain) and Admiral Alonzo Freeman (Vulcan advisory).
- **Psychology**: Anti-authoritarian genius; willfully avoiding promotion; best operative on the ship when motivated.
- **Key arcs**: Mariner/Boimler friendship; Locarno arc (S4 "Old Friends, New Planets"); eventually accepts growth.

### Brad Boimler
- **Played by**: Jack Quaid
- **Rank**: Ensign → Lt. JG (promoted and demoted)
- **Background**: From the transporter duplicate William (in "Kayshon, His Eyes Open" S2E3). Desperate for promotion; by-the-book; sycophantic initially; genuine heroism later.
- **Signature**: "I've been so good!"; panic attacks; admiration for Riker (meets Riker in finale S1)

### D'Vana Tendi
- **Played by**: Noël Wells
- **Rank**: Ensign (Medical) → Science Officer
- **Background**: Orion; her family is literal pirate royalty (hidden from Starfleet); doctor-turned-scientist arc.
- **Signature**: "Oh my gosh!" cheerfulness; Orion pirate reveal in S4

### Sam Rutherford
- **Played by**: Eugene Cordero
- **Rank**: Ensign (Engineering)
- **Background**: Cybernetic implant; partial amnesia (original personality overwritten). S3 reveals the implant's origin conspiracy.

### Captain Carol Freeman
- **Played by**: Dawnn Lewis
- **Background**: Captain of the *Cerritos*; Mariner's mother; falsely arrested for blowing up Pakled Planet in S2 finale.

### Jack Ransom
- **Played by**: Jerry O'Connell
- **Background**: First officer; hyper-masculine Riker pastiche with surprising depth; S2 reveals surprising kindness.

### Shaxs
- **Played by**: Fred Tatasciore
- **Background**: Bajoran security chief; intense; dies in S1 ("No Small Parts"), revived via Vulcan resurrection in S2 ("We'll Always Have Tom Paris").

### Dr. T'Ana
- **Played by**: Gillian Vigman
- **Background**: Caitian CMO; foul-mouthed, grumpy; romantic relationship with Shaxs.

### Billups
- **Played by**: Paul Scheer
- **Background**: Chief engineer; son of the Queen of Hysperia; must remain celibate to avoid inheriting throne.

### Migleemo
- **Played by**: Paul F. Tompkins
- **Background**: Avian counselor; food-metaphor therapist.
