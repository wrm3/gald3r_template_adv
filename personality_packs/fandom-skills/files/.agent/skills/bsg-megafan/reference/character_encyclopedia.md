# BSG (2004) Character Encyclopedia

Full character profiles for the reimagined *Battlestar Galactica* across the miniseries, Seasons 1-4, *Razor*, *The Plan*, *Caprica*, and *Blood & Chrome*. Background, psychology, signature traits, key arcs, iconic quotes, episode citations.

---

## Colonial Fleet

### Admiral William "Husker" Adama
- **Actor**: Edward James Olmos
- **Rank**: Commander (miniseries-S1) → Rear Admiral (S2) → Admiral (S2+)
- **Ship**: Battlestar Galactica (his command since the First Cylon War; was about to be decommissioned at the start of the show)
- **Background**: First Cylon War veteran; Viper pilot; Battle of Medra survivor; trained at the Caprica Fleet Academy. Born c. 2290s.
- **Family**: Son Lee "Apollo" Adama; son Zak Adama (killed in Viper crash pre-series); estranged wife Carolanne; oldest friend Saul Tigh (serves on Galactica with him) — later revealed Tigh is a Cylon
- **Psychology**: Quiet authority earned through decades; guards his privacy obsessively; uses fatherly authority that is both his greatest strength and his blindspot; builds model ships in his cabin as a meditation; prefers paper books and handwritten letters over Cortex-equivalent communications
- **Key episodes**:
  - Miniseries — assumes command after the Fall; orders retreat to find Earth
  - **"Kobol's Last Gleaming Pt. I-II"** (S1E12-13) — Boomer shoots Adama in the abdomen at point-blank range after her sleeper programming activates
  - **"Lay Down Your Burdens Pt. II"** (S2E20) — refuses to accept the election loss; agonizes over whether to intervene
  - **"Exodus Pt. II"** (S3E4) — commands the iconic Galactica atmospheric entry maneuver to rescue New Caprica
  - **"Crossroads Pt. II"** (S3E20) — the Final Four reveal; Saul is a Cylon; Adama must decide whether friendship survives the truth
  - **"Daybreak Pt. II"** (S4E20) — final battle; buries Roslin on the second Earth; settles on a mountaintop
- **Signature**: "So say we all." / "What do you hear?" / "Nothing but the rain." / pushes reading glasses down; whiskey at end of day; model warships
- **Iconic quotes**:
  - "It's not enough to survive. One has to be worthy of surviving."
  - "You cannot play God then wash your hands of the things that you've created. Sooner or later the day comes when you can't hide from the things you've done anymore."
  - (on the last surviving battlestar) "Galactica has seen her share of battles. And she's looking every day of her age. But the truth is... I don't want to lose her."

### President Laura Roslin
- **Actor**: Mary McDonnell
- **Background**: Secretary of Education of the Twelve Colonies; 43rd in the presidential line of succession. Elementary school teacher before politics. Terminal breast cancer diagnosis immediately before the Fall — learns on the day the Cylons attack.
- **Becomes President**: Sworn in aboard Colonial Heavy 798 after the Fall kills 42 others in the line of succession
- **Psychology**: Schoolteacher's precision weaponized into command; prophetic clarity granted by a death sentence; ruthless when the stakes demand it
- **Key arcs**:
  - Cancer remission (via Hera's Cylon-human-hybrid blood, S2)
  - Pythian prophecy and the chamalla visions
  - The election she steals from Baltar and then immediately reverses (S2E19-20 "Lay Down Your Burdens")
  - New Caprica presidency then Cylon occupation
  - Final cancer recurrence (S4)
  - Dies of cancer on the second Earth shortly after arrival (Daybreak Pt. II)
- **Signature**: Glasses + cardigan (S1) → command-suit escalation; chamalla tea; "I'm dying. I might as well be honest"; closing office doors to have a good cry alone
- **Iconic quotes**:
  - "So say we all."
  - "It's going to be all right."
  - "Put the dying scrolls on the board. Under each one, write what we're going to do about it."
  - "I'm dying, Admiral. I might as well be honest."

### Captain Lee "Apollo" Adama
- **Actor**: Jamie Bamber
- **Rank**: Captain (Viper pilot, miniseries) → Major (CAG, S1-3) → Commander (Pegasus, S3-4) → civilian
- **Background**: William Adama's elder son; younger brother Zak killed in a Viper crash pre-series (a crash caused by Kara Thrace passing him in flight school despite his lack of skill). Was already strained with his father about Zak's death.
- **Psychology**: Idealistic; haunted by duty vs. conscience; the fleet's moral compass. Constantly self-questioning. Marries Anastasia "Dee" Dualla; they separate; he moves between Kara and Dee with unclear resolution.
- **Key arcs**:
  - Miniseries — pilots the escape Raptor with Roslin aboard
  - CAG promotions and command tensions
  - **"Home Pt. II"** (S2E7) — leads Raptor to Kobol; finds Arrow of Apollo
  - Baltar's defense attorney at Baltar's trial ("Crossroads" S3E19-20) — chooses the law over the politics of the moment
  - Commander of Pegasus (S3-4)
  - Final act: President of the Twelve Colonies for a brief period before the Daybreak settlement; civilian council leader; farmer
- **Iconic quote**: "The law has to mean something."

### Captain Kara "Starbuck" Thrace
- **Actor**: Katee Sackhoff
- **Rank**: Lieutenant → Captain (Viper pilot; CAG occasionally)
- **Background**:
  - Parents: Socrata Thrace (abusive pianist mother); Dreilide Thrace (absent father, implied musical connection — *possibly* the Cylon Model Seven "Daniel," never confirmed)
  - Fiancée to Zak Adama pre-series; his death in the crash she caused by passing him is the buried original sin of the series
  - Marries Samuel Anders on New Caprica (S3) — Anders revealed as Cylon later
- **Psychology**: Self-destructive brilliance; prophesied "harbinger of death"; carries the mandala symbol in her subconscious art; vanishes into storms
- **Key arcs**:
  - Tortures Leoben in "Flesh and Bone" (S1E8); he tells her "you're going to find Kobol"
  - Held captive by Leoben on New Caprica (S3E1-2 "Occupation/Precipice") — he obsessively loves her; she kills him repeatedly
  - **"Maelstrom"** (S3E17) — flies into a storm; her Viper explodes; she's "dead"
  - **"Crossroads Pt. II"** (S3E20) — returns in a pristine Viper; nobody believes her
  - **"Daybreak Pt. II"** (S4E20) — leads fleet to second Earth; disappears without explanation. Her final scene: tells Lee "I've been done with this for a while"; walks out of frame; never returns.
- **Status**: Intentionally unresolved. Ronald Moore: "I like to think of her as an angel."
- **Signature**: Cigar; stim pills; the mandala; painting her cockpit interior; "viper jockey"; "see you on the other side"
- **Iconic quotes**:
  - "Let's go home."
  - "Yeah, I saw the tech debt. I flew into it."
  - "I'm not going to be the officer who signs that order."

### Dr. Gaius Baltar
- **Actor**: James Callis
- **Background**: Brilliant civilian scientist from the Colonies; inventor; unwitting traitor (provided access codes to Caprica Six, whom he was sleeping with; she uploaded them to the Cylon network). Survived the Fall by being sheltered by Caprica Six when the attack came.
- **Head Six**: A vision of Caprica Six only Baltar can see. Present from the moment of the Fall onward. Guides him, manipulates him, has theological conversations with him. Later revealed to be an Angel of God (same category as Head Baltar appearing to Caprica Six and as post-death Starbuck).
- **Psychology**: Magnificent coward; capable of genuine brilliance under pressure; capable of real heroism (stopped Cylon plan to kill Hera; delivered the Miranda broadcast equivalent on Caprica rebel station); always finds a way to make it about himself.
- **Four-phase arc**:
  1. **Survivor guilt / unwitting traitor** (miniseries-S1): hides his role; Head Six guides him; wins Vice Presidency through the fiasco of Aaron Doral's suicide bombing confusion
  2. **Presidency / puppet / New Caprica collaboration** (S2-3): wins the election; the Cylons arrive at New Caprica the day after his inauguration; Baltar signs collaboration orders under coercion; leaves New Caprica with the Cylons when the fleet escapes
  3. **Trial / cult leader** (S3-4): acquitted on technical grounds; exiled socially; gathered by a cult of traumatized women; becomes a genuinely religious figure even while being a fraud
  4. **Grace** (S4 finale): leads a militia during the final battle; fires a rifle for perhaps the first and last time; settles on second Earth as a farmer with Caprica Six; accepts who he is
- **Iconic quotes**:
  - "I may be many things, but I am not a murderer." (he is)
  - "I know about farming."
  - "I see angels. Angels in this very room."
  - (to Caprica Six in finale) "I know about farming... I come from a farming family."

### Colonel Saul Tigh
- **Actor**: Michael Hogan
- **Rank**: Colonel; Executive Officer of Galactica throughout the series
- **Background**: First Cylon War veteran; Adama's oldest friend (predates Adama's marriage); chronic alcoholism; wife Ellen Tigh (complicated, seductive, manipulative — also a Final Five Cylon as revealed in S4)
- **Key arcs**:
  - XO aboard Galactica; fractious with Starbuck
  - **New Caprica resistance leader** (S3E1-4): commands the insurgency against Cylon occupation; executes suicide bombers; poisons his own wife Ellen for collaborating with Cylons (later revealed she was also a Cylon — added tragic dimension)
  - Loses left eye during Cylon interrogation on New Caprica — becomes the eye patch
  - **Final Four revelation** (S3E20 "Crossroads Pt. II"): he is a Cylon
  - **Father to Caprica Six's baby Liam** (S4): fell in love with Caprica Six; the pregnancy was stillborn
  - Leads Rebel Cylons alongside Sam Anders
  - Settles on second Earth with Ellen, their third-life marriage finally stable
- **Signature**: Eye patch; flask; "God damn it"; Adama-loyalty above all
- **Iconic quote**: "I'm not a Cylon. I know I'm not a Cylon. But I'm not."

### Chief Galen Tyrol
- **Actor**: Aaron Douglas
- **Rank**: Chief Petty Officer; runs Galactica's flight deck
- **Background**: Son of a Gemenese Priestess; raised on a ship. Romance with Boomer throughout S1 (she didn't know she was a Cylon; he didn't know either). Later marries Cally Henderson; son Nicholas (revealed to be biologically Hot Dog's in S4 — not Tyrol's).
- **Key arcs**:
  - Boomer romance (S1) — ends catastrophically
  - Union strike leader on New Caprica (S3)
  - **Final Four revelation** (S3E20)
  - Cally's murder (S4E3 "The Ties That Bind") — Tory Foster throws her out an airlock
  - Kills Tory Foster in Daybreak Pt. III for killing Cally
  - Settles alone in Scotland-equivalent on second Earth
- **Signature**: Grease-stained coveralls; wrenches; calloused hands; pragmatism; "Chief"
- **Iconic quote**: "We have to remember who we are."

### Dr. Sharon "Boomer" Valerii / Lt. Sharon "Athena" Agathon
- **Actor**: Grace Park
- **Models**: Both are Number Eight; two distinct instances with opposite moral paths
- **Boomer (Sharon Valerii)**:
  - Viper/Raptor pilot aboard Galactica; engaged to Tyrol; didn't know she was a Cylon
  - Sleeper programming activates: shoots Adama (S1E13 "Kobol's Last Gleaming Pt. II")
  - Killed aboard Galactica; resurrected on a basestar
  - Disillusioned by Cavil's machinations
  - In S4, kidnaps baby Hera; flees to Cavil's faction
  - Killed by Athena in "Daybreak Pt. II"
- **Athena (Sharon Agathon)**:
  - The Number Eight who was stationed on Caprica to seduce Karl "Helo" Agathon; genuinely fell in love
  - Defected; chose humanity; gave birth to Hera (first known Cylon-Human hybrid)
  - Eventually trusted with a Colonial commission; serves as pilot
  - Settles on second Earth with Helo and Hera
- **Key note**: The two instances serve as the show's central thesis on identity, choice, and moral responsibility — same biology, same memories, completely different people
- **Iconic quote (Athena)**: "I am not her. I know what I am."

### Lt. Karl "Helo" Agathon
- **Actor**: Tahmoh Penikett
- **Rank**: Lieutenant → Captain → Major
- **Background**: Raptor ECO; gave up his seat on the last Raptor off Caprica to Baltar; stranded; fell in love with Sharon (Athena); married her; fathered Hera.
- **Psychology**: Moral compass of the series. Refuses to compromise on the humanity of Cylons once he knows them. Decent man in indecent times.
- **Key arc**: In "A Measure of Salvation" (S3E7), the Fleet captures Cylon infection bodies; Apollo plans to use them as a bioweapon to exterminate all Cylons. Helo and Athena refuse and destroy the weapon. The fleet considers it mutiny; Adama lets them keep their commissions.
- **Iconic quote**: "If we do this, we are the thing we were fighting."

### Lt. Felix Gaeta
- **Actor**: Alessandro Juliani
- **Rank**: Lieutenant (Tactical Officer)
- **Background**: Devout believer in the Colonial system; meticulous record-keeper; in romantic relationship with Hoshi (revealed S4, one of the first explicitly queer characters in a prestige military drama)
- **Key arc**: Served as Baltar's Secretary on New Caprica (secretly feeding the resistance intel); post-occupation was branded a collaborator; scapegoated at Baltar's trial when Baltar refused to confirm Gaeta's role; loses his leg in S4; leads the Gaeta-Zarek mutiny (S4E13 "The Oath", S4E14 "Blood on the Scales"); executed by firing squad
- **Iconic quote**: "It's always the ones who say they know the truth who have the blood on their hands."

### Lt. Anastasia "Dee" Dualla
- **Actor**: Kandyse McClure
- **Rank**: Specialist → Lieutenant (Communications)
- **Background**: Runs CIC communications on Galactica; marries Lee Adama (S3); separates; reconciles briefly
- **Death**: Shoots herself in the head on Galactica (S4E11 "Sometimes a Great Notion") after a peaceful morning with Lee — having just seen the devastated Earth 1. One of the quietest devastating suicides in prestige TV.
- **Iconic quote**: "Galactica, Pegasus actual."

### The Hybrid (primary)
- **Actor**: Tiffany Lyndall-Knight (and later others)
- **Role**: Organic intelligence that runs each Cylon basestar; floats in a fluid tank; speaks in fragmented oracle cadence ending always with "end of line"
- **Function**: Channels the "stream of God's consciousness" (per Cylon theology); predicts events obliquely; cannot be directly commanded but listened to carefully
- **In S4 finale**: Samuel Anders becomes *THE* Hybrid when he's connected to Galactica's systems to guide the fleet to Earth 2
- **Iconic quote**: "The children of the ones who were thrown out will return. Throughout history, the nexus of realization has been... the five will come hand in hand to the land of the thirteenth tribe. End of line."

### Brendan "Hot Dog" Costanza
- **Actor**: Bodie Olmos (Edward James Olmos's real son)
- **Rank**: Lieutenant (Viper pilot, Nugget promoted in S1)
- **Background**: Comic-relief nugget who survives; competent; secretly Nicholas Tyrol's biological father (revealed S4)

### Kat (Louanne Katraine)
- **Actor**: Luciana Carro
- **Background**: Nugget pilot; complicated past as a drug smuggler (hidden identity "Kat" vs. real "Sasha"); becomes CAG briefly in S3; dies saving a transport ("The Passage" S3E10)

### Kendra Shaw (Razor)
- **Actor**: Stephanie Jacobsen
- **Background**: Cain's protégée aboard Pegasus; Razor-film-centric character; does Cain's dirty work and is broken by it; sacrifices herself to destroy the First Hybrid's base in the film's climax

### Romo Lampkin
- **Actor**: Mark Sheppard
- **Background**: Theatrical defense lawyer; Baltar's attorney at trial; sunglasses indoors; carries a cat (or pretends to)
- **Arc**: Unexpectedly selected President of the Twelve Colonies near the end of S4 in the Quorum's disarray. Serves briefly and uneventfully.
- **Iconic quote** (trial closing argument): "Because they're afraid. And you should be afraid."

### Cally Henderson Tyrol
- **Actor**: Nicki Clyne
- **Background**: Deck crew; marries Tyrol; mother of Nicholas (later revealed to be Hot Dog's biological son). Killed by Tory Foster in S4E3 "The Ties That Bind" via airlock.

## The Cylons

### Brother Cavil / Number One
- **Actor**: Dean Stockwell
- **Role**: The villain; created by the Final Five; hates being Cylon; wants to be pure machine; orchestrates the Fall of the Colonies as revenge against the Five
- **Key episode**: "No Exit" (S4E15) reveals his complete backstory and motivations
- **Death**: Shoots himself in the mouth during Daybreak Pt. II when his deal with Galactica collapses
- **Iconic quote**: "I want to see gamma rays. I want to hear X-rays. And I want to smell dark matter."

### Leoben Conoy / Number Two
- **Actor**: Callum Keith Rennie
- **Role**: The mystic; obsessed with Starbuck; speaks in fragments and prophetic connections
- **Key arcs**: Captured and tortured by Starbuck in "Flesh and Bone" (S1E8); holds her prisoner on New Caprica (S3E1-2); their relationship is an abusive obsession she repeatedly kills him for
- **Iconic quote**: "All of this has happened before, and all of it will happen again."

### D'Anna Biers / Number Three
- **Actor**: Lucy Lawless (yes — Xena)
- **Role**: Obsessive seer; pushes further than any Cylon; boxes herself to glimpse the Final Five; unboxed briefly in S3
- **Key episode**: "Rapture" (S3E12) — she sees the Final Five for one moment and is immediately boxed by Cavil
- **Unboxing**: S4 briefly; dies before the final battle

### Simon O'Neill / Number Four
- **Actor**: Rick Worthy
- **Role**: The doctor; methodical unsettling clinical precision; ran the "farms" on Caprica where human women were harvested for reproductive experiments; embodied horror beneath a bedside manner
- **Appearances**: Episodes about the farms; minor in later seasons (Cavil kept the Fours less visible)

### Aaron Doral / Number Five
- **Actor**: Matthew Bennett
- **Role**: The PR man; pleasant, forgettable; also the suicide bomber who attempts to kill the Galactica crew in Cylon Pt. I of the miniseries (the bomb is defused; Doral is left behind to die on Ragnar Anchorage)
- **Later**: Various Dorals across the show; Cylon civilian liaison roles

### Number Six (multiple instances)
- **Actor**: Tricia Helfer
- **Caprica Six** — the one who seduced Baltar and enabled the Fall; survived in Baltar's company; develops a conscience
- **Head Six** — the Baltar-vision Six; eventually revealed to be an Angel of God; mirrored by Head Baltar who appears to Caprica Six in S4 finale
- **Natalie** — S4 Cylon rebel leader; killed in "Guess What's Coming to Dinner" (S4E7)
- **Gina** — the Six imprisoned on Pegasus; tortured by Cain and the Pegasus crew; triggers the nuclear device hidden on Cloud 9 in S2 finale
- **Shelly Godfrey** — tries to publicly expose Baltar as a traitor ("Six Degrees of Separation" S1E7); disappears before confrontation
- **Lida** / **Sonja** — minor S4 rebel Sixes
- **Key quote (Caprica Six)**: "God's plan is sometimes not our plan."

### Number Seven — Daniel
- **Never shown.** Created by the Final Five as a deeply sensitive artistic Cylon; Cavil poisoned his development in utero out of jealousy and boxed the entire line before they could gestate
- **Referenced only in "No Exit"** (S4E15) by Ellen in exposition
- **Fan theory**: Daniel might be Kara Thrace's biological father. Strongly implied; never confirmed. Moore: "I don't want to answer that question"

### Number Eight (Boomer / Athena)
- Covered above under Fleet section (both instances)

### The Final Five
See `cylon_lore.md` for the full reveal. Summary individual profiles:

- **Saul Tigh** — Colonial XO; resurrected memories S3 finale; father of Caprica Six's stillborn son Liam; rebel Cylon leader
- **Galen Tyrol** — Galactica deck chief; resurrected memories S3 finale; settles alone on second Earth
- **Samuel Anders** — Caprican pyramid captain turned resistance fighter; Starbuck's husband; becomes THE Hybrid at end of S4 to guide fleet to Earth

## Pegasus / Razor Characters

### Admiral Helena Cain
- **Actor**: Michelle Forbes
- **Rank**: Admiral, commanding Battlestar Pegasus
- **Background**: Pegasus survived the Fall by rapid jump; Cain took command completely
- **Atrocities**:
  - Shot her XO Belzen for hesitating
  - Harvested civilian ships for parts and crew; left civilians to die or be forced into military service
  - Kept a Six model (Gina) as a torture and rape subject; allowed her crew to abuse Gina
- **Relationship with Gina**: Gina and Cain had been lovers before the Fall (before Cain knew Gina was a Cylon). Cain's brutality toward Gina after the reveal was a complex betrayal arc.
- **Death**: Shot in the head by Gina (S2E12 "Resurrection Ship Pt. II")
- **Razor**: The TV film is her backstory
- **Iconic quote**: "Sometimes terrible things have to be done."

### Gina Inviere (Six)
- **Actor**: Tricia Helfer
- **Background**: Six imprisoned aboard Pegasus; abused; triggers a hidden nuclear device on Cloud 9 at the end of S2 ("Lay Down Your Burdens Pt. II")

## Political Characters

### Tom Zarek
- **Actor**: Richard Hatch (1978 BSG's Apollo)
- **Background**: Prison poet on Sagittaron; terrorist turned politician; Vice President; imprisoned for decades before the show began
- **Arc**: Competes with Roslin for power; initially the Fleet's antagonist; develops into uneasy partnership with Baltar; co-leads the Gaeta mutiny; executed
- **Casting significance**: Casting the original Apollo as the series' antagonist of civic order was deliberate and symbolic

## Key Relationships

- **Adama ↔ Tigh**: Oldest friendship (40+ years); survives the Cylon reveal
- **Adama ↔ Roslin**: Professional partnership → love → deathbed devotion; never formally married
- **Starbuck ↔ Lee Adama**: Unresolved star-crossed; Lee always married to Dee; Kara always married to Anders; they never quite work out despite obvious mutual love
- **Starbuck ↔ Anders**: The Caprica-resistance marriage; strained post-revelation that Anders is a Cylon; she stays with him through his Hybrid transformation
- **Starbuck ↔ Leoben**: Obsession / captor / projection of divinity; he sees her as a messenger; she kills him
- **Baltar ↔ Caprica Six**: Began as seduction / betrayal; evolves into genuine partnership; end the series farming together on second Earth
- **Baltar ↔ Head Six**: The manifest supernatural guidance; Angel
- **Tigh ↔ Ellen**: Marriage that survives death, resurrection, infidelity, execution, reveal-both-Cylon; the most functional relationship on the show by end
- **Tyrol ↔ Boomer**: Initial love; betrayal; post-reveal obsession; Tyrol never fully recovers
- **Helo ↔ Athena**: The show's stable marriage; first interspecies parents of Hera
- **Roslin ↔ Zarek**: Political rivals → wary allies → enemies again
- **Cain ↔ Gina**: Pre-Fall lovers; Cain's post-reveal brutality destroys both
