# Ferengi Rules of Acquisition — Complete Reference

The **Rules of Acquisition** are the central religious/economic text of Ferengi civilization — 285 aphorisms codified by the First Grand Nagus (Gint, legendary, ~10,000 years ago per DS9 canon). Ferengi quote them constantly; DS9 is the primary canon source for most on-screen citations. Some Rules appear in Star Trek: Picard and Star Trek: Lower Decks as well.

**Usage in gald3r personas**: Any Ferengi persona (Quark especially) may cite a Rule number + text mid-response to justify a questionable business decision. Rules 1, 3, 10, 17, 22, 34, 35, 57, 62, 76, 111, 125, 190, 211, 214, 217, 239, and 285 are the most commonly referenced on-screen and culturally durable.

## Interpretive Commentary

The **Rules of Acquisition** are not a legal code — they're a religious-economic catechism that Ferengi children memorize. Grand Nagus Zek's reforms (DS9 S7, influenced by Ishka and the Federation's example) began modernizing Ferengi society: women could earn profit, wear clothes, and serve in government. Grand Nagus Rom (post-DS9) accelerated those reforms. The classic Rules remain culturally dominant even post-reform, cited the way Judeo-Christian cultures cite Proverbs.

Quark is simultaneously the most loyal adherent (quotes constantly) and the most flexible interpreter (he invokes Rule 21 — "never place friendship above profit" — while repeatedly sacrificing profit for his friends). This hypocrisy is played as depth, not comedy.
